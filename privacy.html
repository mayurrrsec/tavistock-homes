01 Introduction

This policy explains how Tavistock Homes Ltd (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses and protects personal information in connection with this website and our residential children's home service. We are committed to protecting the privacy, dignity and safety of the young people in our care, their families, and everyone who contacts us.

02 Who we are

Tavistock Homes Ltd is the “data controller” for the personal information we hold. If you have any questions about this policy or how we handle your information, please contact us at admin@tavistockhomes.com or +44 115 919 4258.

03 The information we collect

  • This website does not use contact forms and does not collect personal information for marketing.
  • If you contact us by phone or email, we collect the details you choose to share — such as your name, contact details and the nature of your enquiry — so that we can respond.
  • If you make a referral, we receive and process information about the young person and the referral, provided by placing authorities and the professionals involved.

04 How we use your information

We use personal information to respond to enquiries, to assess and manage referrals, and to provide safe, high-quality care. Our lawful bases include our legitimate interests (responding to you), our legal obligations, and the performance of a task carried out in the public interest as a registered children's home.

05 Referral & children's data

Information about the young people in our care is treated with the utmost confidentiality and stored securely. We process it to provide safe, high-quality care and to meet our legal and regulatory obligations, including the Children's Homes (England) Regulations 2015. Access is restricted to staff who need it, and we share it only with the relevant authorities and professionals where required.

06 Cookies & the map

Our website does not set its own cookies. Some pages include an embedded Google Map to show our general location. When the map loads, Google may set cookies on your device and process data in line with Google's own privacy policy. You can control or block cookies through your browser settings; blocking them may affect how the map displays.

07 Sharing your information

We do not sell personal information. We share it only where necessary — for example with placing local authorities, health and education partners, and regulators such as Ofsted — and only where we have a lawful basis to do so.

08 How long we keep it

We keep personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, and in line with our retention schedule and the statutory requirements that apply to children's homes records.

09 Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • access the personal information we hold about you;
  • ask us to correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete;
  • ask us to erase information, or restrict or object to its processing, in certain circumstances;
  • ask for a copy of your information in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent where we rely on it.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

10 Contact & complaints

If you have any concerns about how we handle your information, please contact us first at admin@tavistockhomes.com. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with a revised date.
